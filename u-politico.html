OVERVIEW

After long periods of successive dominance by Poland-Lithuania, Russia, and the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (U.S.S.R.), Ukraine finally achieved full independence in the 20th century. Ukraine had experienced a brief period of independence in 1918â€“20, but parts of western Ukraine were ruled by Poland, Romania, and Czechoslovakia in the period between the two World Wars, and Ukraine subsequently joined the Soviet Union as the Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic (December 1, 1991). JUSTICE

The legal system of Ukraine is unified. Both the adversary and inquisitory justice philosophies are the foundation of its criminal justice system. While initial inquiries adopt an inquisitory strategy, the adversarial principle takes center stage when the case is tried.

POLITICAL PROCESS

Voting rights are available to citizens who are at least 18 years old. The Communist Party of Ukraine (CPU), a division of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, was the only recognized political party in Ukraine up until 1990. Significant laws that were approved by the Ukrainian Supreme Soviet came from or were authorized by the CPU. New political parties were given official recognition in October 1990 after the Ukrainian constitution was changed. A diverse range of parties then developed. However, many parties have lacked solid organizational foundations and unifying platforms, and as a result, individual parties have a tendency to create parliamentary blocs.

FOREIGN EXCHANGE CONTROLS (DUE TO THE CURRENT WAR SITUATION)

  • The National Bank of Ukraine published guidelines on how the financial system and foreign exchange market should operate while martial law is in effect on February 24, 2022. (the Resolution)
  • The NBU' s efforts are intended to guarantee the ongoing operation of vital infrastructure facilities, maximum assistance for the Ukrainian Armed Forces, and the unhindered operation of the nation ' s financial system.
  • The declaration of martial law in Ukraine has resulted in the following temporary restrictions:
  • With the exception of transactions involving sales of foreign currencies, the Ukrainian currency market is not operating.
  • The creation of e-money, the addition of e-money to electronic wallets, and the distribution of e-money are all on hold.
  • The serving banks of the Russian Federation stop performing debit transactions on the accounts of its citize
  • As of February 24, 2022, the official UAH exchange rate has been set.
  • Cash from clients ' accounts in foreign currencies is restricted and withdrawals from each individual client account are capped at UAH 100,000 per day.
  • Payments made in foreign currencies across borders are now prohibited.
  • Debit transactions from service banks on the accounts of Russian Federation residents have been halted.
  • Enterprises and institutions that provide mobilization plans (tasks) and the Government of individual approvals by the NBU are exempt from these restrictions.
  • Additionally, the Resolution calls for the following actions:
  • In accordance with the limitations outlined in this Regulation, banks must ensure continuous functioning and uninterrupted access to safe deposit boxes;
  • Non-cash payments are not constrained;
  • ATMs are unrestrictedly backed by cash;
  • The NBU engages in limitless cash backing;
  • The NBU carries out blank refinancing for banks to preserve liquidity with no caps on the amounts for up to a year, with the option of an extension for another year;
  • For the duration of the special legislation, payments will be made by the Ukrainian government without restriction
